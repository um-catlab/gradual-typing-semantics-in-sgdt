Proof of Lemma \ref{lem:run-fill}:
\begin{proof}
  
    In the below:
    %
    \begin{itemize}
        \item We let $\eta$ denote $T_{\mho}.\eta$ and $\eta'$ denote $T_{\mho sb}.\eta$.
        %
        \item We let $(-)^\dag$ denote Kleisli extension for $T_{\mho}$ and
        $(-)^\ddag$ denote Kleisli extension for $T_{\mho sb}$.
        %
        \item $f : (\term{A} + \Sigma_\mho(\term{A})) \to T_{\mho}(X)$ is
        defined by cases, mapping $\cpure{M}$ to $\eta^{T_{\mho}}(M)$, and
        mapping $\ceff{(\oper, u, g)}$ to 
        $T_{\mho}(\term{A}).\oper(\eta \circ u, (\later \eta) \circ g)$.
        %
        (Note that $u : \tau_\oper \to \term{A}$ and $g : \gamma_\oper \to \later (\term A)$.)
        %
        \item $\iota : T_{\mho}(X) \to T_{\mho sb}(X)$ is the inclusion of the
        free model monad $T_{\mho}$ into $T_{\mho sb}$. 
    \end{itemize}

    We want to show that $\run{(E[M])} = (\run{E})(\run{M})$. We proceed by
    guarded recursion and cases on whether $M$ is a value, or $M$ steps.

    If $M$ is a value $V$, then by definition of $\run$ on terms, we have
    $\run{V} = \eta\, V$.

    Now suppose $M \stepsin{k} \hat{N}$, where $\hat{N} : \Sigmahat(\term A)$.
    Then we have, by definition of $\run{}$:

    \begin{equation} 
        \run{M} = \delta_{\sty} (\run^{\ddag} {(\iota(f(\hat{N})))}).
    \end{equation}

    We also have $E[M] \stepsin{k} \Sigmahat(E[\cdot])(\hat{N})$. Thus, by the
    definition of $\run{}$, we have:
    \begin{equation} 
        \run{(E[M])} = \delta_{\sty} (\run^{\ddag} {(\iota(f (\Sigmahat(E[\cdot])(\hat{N})) ))}).
    \end{equation}
    
    Now, we have:
    \begin{align*}
        \run{(E[M])} 
        &= \delta_{\sty} [\run^{\ddag} {(\iota(f (\Sigmahat(E[\cdot])(\hat{N})) ))}] \\
        &= \delta_{\sty} [\run^{\ddag} {((\eta' \circ E[\cdot])^{\ddag} (\iota(f(\hat{N})))) }] \\
        &= \delta_{\sty} [(\run^{\ddag} \circ (\eta' \circ E[\cdot]))^{\ddag} (\iota(f(\hat{N}))) ] \\
        &= \delta_{\sty} [(\run \circ E[\cdot])^{\ddag} (\iota(f(\hat{N}))) ] \\
        &= \delta_{\sty} [(\lambda M. \run {(E[M])})^{\ddag} (\iota(f(\hat{N}))) ]. \\
    \end{align*}

    On the other hand, we have
    \begin{align*}
        (\run E)(\run M) 
        &= (\lambda V. \run{(E[V])})^{\ddag} (\delta_{\sty} (\run^{\ddag} {(\iota(f(\hat{N})))}) ) \\
        &= \delta_{\sty} [(\lambda V. \run{(E[V])})^{\ddag} (\run^{\ddag} {(\iota(f(\hat{N})))})] \\
        &= \delta_{\sty} [((\lambda V. \run{(E[V])})^{\ddag} \circ \run)^{\ddag} {(\iota(f(\hat{N})))}] \\
        &= \delta_{\sty} [(\lambda M. ((\run E) (\run M)))^{\ddag} {(\iota(f(\hat{N})))}]. \\
    \end{align*}

    So it suffices to show that the final lines of the above two chains of
    equalities are equal. Since both sides are guarded by a $\delta_{\sty}$, it
    suffices to show that the insides are equal later. This follows from our
    \lob induction hypothesis.

    %     = ((\lambda V. \run{(E[V])})^{\ddag} \circ \run)^{\ddag} {(\iota(f(\hat{N})))} ]. 
    % \]


\end{proof}

Proof of Lemma \ref{lem:error-monad-transformer}:
\begin{proof}
    Let $\calA = (\Sigma_\calA, E_\calA)$ be a guarded algebraic theory with
    free-model monad given by $T_\calA$. Let $T'(A) = T(A + 1)$.

    We first show that $T'(A)$ implements each operation in $\calA +
    \th{\mho}$. We define 
    %
    \[ \sem{\mho} = T.\eta(\inr\, *). \]
    %
    For each operation $\oper : (\tau, \gamma)$ in $A$, we define $\sem{\oper} :
    ((\tau_\oper \to T'(A)) \times (\gamma_\oper \to \later (T'(A)))) \to T'(A)$
    using the fact that $T(A+1)$ implements the operations, i.e, we define
    %
    \[ \sem{\oper}(u, g) = T(A+1).\sem{\oper}(u, g) \]
    
    We define the inclusion of generators $\eta' : A \to T'(A)$ by
    %
    \[ \eta'(x) = T(A+1).\eta(\inl\, x). \]

    Next, we show that the equations are satisfied. Since there are no equations
    in $\th{\mho}$, we need only show that the equations of $\calA$ hold.
    Specifically, let $e = (\Gamma, l, r)$ be an equation in $E_\calA$, where
    $l, r$ are terms in the signature $\Sigma_\calA$ with variables in $\Gamma$.
    Consider an arbitrary $f : \Gamma \to T'(A)$. We need to show that
    $\sem{l}_f = \sem{r}_f$. Since we know that $l$ and $r$ do not mention
    $\mho$, we know that this equality holds by the fact that $T(A + 1)$ is a
    model for $\calA$.

    Finally, we show the universal property. Let $B$ be a model for $\calA +
    \th{\mho}$ and let $f : A \to |B|$. We define $\fbar : T'(A) \to |B|$ using
    the universal property of $T(A + 1)$. Namely, since $B$ is also a model for
    $\calA$, it suffices to define a function $g : A + 1 \to |B|$. We define
    %
    \[ g(\inl x) = f(x) \] 
    and
    \[ g(\inr *) = B.\mho. \]
    %
    Then by the universal property this extends uniquely to a function $\ghat :
    T(A + 1) \to |B|$ that preserves the operations in $\calA$.

    Then it is clear that this is a homomorphism of models of $\calA =
    \th{\mho}$ since $g(\sem{\mho}) = \ghat(T.\eta(\inr\, *)) = g(\inr\, *) =
    B.\mho$.

    For uniqueness, suppose $h, h' : T(A + 1) \to B$ are homomorphisms of
    $(\calA + \th{\mho})$-models such that $h \circ \eta' = h' \circ \eta'$. We
    show that $h = h'$. 

\end{proof}
